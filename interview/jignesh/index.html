Jignesh

Short Story

Jignesh Bhai chants “Jay Jalaram” & opens the shutter of his Kirana store every day at sharp 8 am in the morning. Jignesh Bhai runs a small Kirana Store in the Khanderao Market area, Vadodara, Gujarat. His shop standstill in the heart of the city. Jignesh Bhai stays happily with his wife, son & his daughter-in-law in his 2 story building apartment just above his Kirana store. He runs this family-owned business with full involvement from his wife & his son. He is a pure businessman who has been sitting on the Cash counter since age 15.

Basic Details

Q. What is the name of the merchant and his age?

A. The full name of the merchant is Jignesh Chotalal Lankavala aged 55.

Q. Name of the shop and locality?

A. Jignesh Bhai runs a Kirana store in the heart of the city Vadodara- opposite to the Khanderao market. The name of the Kirana store is Lankavala Pannalal Chotalal, which has been named after his family name

Q. Who are the members of his family?

A. The family of Jignesh Bhai consists of a total of four people, his wife, his son & his daughter-in-law. Jignesh Bhai opens the shop at sharp 8 am in the morning & closes at 9 pm. Jignesh Bhai manages the shop from 8 a.m. to 12 pm along with his son. At sharp 12 pm, his wife comes down & sits on the cash counter, meanwhile, both father & son have their lunch. Jignesh Bhai comes down at 3 pm, after his short nap & stays in the store at a stretch till 9 pm.

Basic Business Details

Q. Why did he start his business?

A. Kirana Store of Jignesh Bhai is somewhat 75-80 years old & was started by his father Chotalal at his very young age. This Kirana store is running at the time even before Jignesh Bhai was born. Chotalal started this business because after coming back from his native village Anand, Gujarat as he didn’t want to continue his farming business. Jignesh Bhai feels that an escape from farming was the reason for the establishment of the Kirana store.

Q. When did he start his business?

A. Chotalal Lankavala started this business some 75-80 years ago when he was young & Jignesh Bhai wasn’t born yet.

Q. What does his business deal with?

A. Lankavala Pannalal Chotalal is a Kirana store & sells all consumer goods. It sells almost all FMCG goods under its one roof. Today Chotalal Lankavala has passed away, but Jignesh Bhai considers his father as an idol & whole & sole owner of this. Jignesh Bhai is still thankful to his father who has started this well-established Kirana store from a scratch level. Chotalal used to worship his Kirana store like a goddess, says Jagdish Bhai. Chotalal has taught all business tactics to Jignesh Bhai & now Jignesh Bhai is teaching the same to his son.

Operational Details

Q. How did he manage the initial capital?

A. Chotalal has invested its first-ever capital in the business after selling his inherited land of farming. The exact amount of initial investment is unknown but it was financed after selling inherited agricultural land.

Q. What were the major difficulties in running his business?

A. Jignesh Bhai says that big departmental stores have hampered their businesses directly. Customers prefer big departmental stores like D-mart, More Megastore, etc above local Kirana stores. But Jignesh Bhai has claimed that lockdown due to corona has positively impacted its businesses. Because of the pandemic, customers now prefer local over international

Q. Does he get help from his family members?

A. Jignesh Bhai gets a 100% full support from his wife & son. His son has also now taken a full interest in the business & is looking forward to continuing the legacy after Jignesh Bhai. The shop is never kept empty only with paid workers. One of the other family members is always there.

Q. How much support has he hired?

A. Jignesh Bhai has hired an additional 2 workers for his operational support during pick hours.

Q. Most of the time he is in the shop what does he do & What does he do in his free time?

A. Jignesh Bhai hardly gets any free time in his day time, as his shop is always flooded with customers. If he gets any free any time, then he checks out stock of food products, inventories, accounting, cash balance & other such things Jignesh Bhai says that his day is not like the same for the whole day. The heavy rush can be seen from 9 am to 12 am. He says his business is comparatively silent in the afternoon. All those coming in the afternoon time are majorly buying 1-2 items only. Later pick hours are seen between 5 pm to 9 pm. He says that many times, the store is opened till 10 pm due to a heavy rush. Post-Lockdown also sales have increased due to precautionary measures of going to the big departmental stores.

Customer Details

Q. What are his top priorities?

A. Jignesh Bhai treats all customers the same only. He doesn’t have any discrimination while selling. He believes that a customer buying goods of Rs 10 is equivalent to a customer buying goods of Rs 3000

Q. Who are his typical customers?

A. The customers coming to the Kirana store are majorly from localities around only. Jignesh Bhai says that faraway customers do not generally come. Finance & Technology Details

Q. Does he use a POS Terminal for accepting payments, if yes, why, if no, why not?

A. Jignesh Bhai uses POS Terminal & accepts all types of card payments. He has started using the POS system for the past 6-7 years only. According to Jignesh Bhai, almost 50% of customers use the POS terminal for accepting payments. His son has played an important role in making his adverse in using financial technology.

Q. What is the %age of cash transactions at his shop?

A. Cash transaction has gradually reduced to 25-30% over the years, says Jignesh Bhai. Customers coming to buy goods worth Rs 1000 most of the time prefer card or UPI payments. Cash transactions are more in quantities but in monetary value, the percentage contribution to the turnover is very less.

Q. What is the %age of QR code payments at his shop?

A. Almost 25-30% of transactions are made through QR code. Jignesh Bhai accepts Gpay & Phonepe only. He doesn’t accept Paytm payments from the customers. His son looks at QR code payments, as Jignesh Bhai is not very comfortable with it.

Q. Does he use an ATM? Why does he withdraw money from the ATM?

A. Jignesh Bhai uses an ATM card to withdraw cash, only when he has to pay to its suppliers. The frequency of going to ATM machines is very less.

Q. How much money does he make in a month?

A. Jignesh Bhai approximately earns a revenue of Rs 1,00,000 every month, after deducting all its expenses. The revenues have increased after his son joined the business. His son picks up the inventory most of the time from supplier, thus was successful in maintaining a good relationship with suppliers, resultantly high discounts on bulk orders are procured by them.

Q. Does he take a loan from external people for running his shop? If yes, what is the interest he pays?

A. Jignesh Bhai has not procured any loans yet.

Q. Has he registered for GST?

A. As most of the suppliers have their GSTIN number, Jignesh Bhai has also registered for the same to get cash credit. Thus making it a GST registered Kirana store

Q. How much does he pay monthly for electricity, phone, gas, shop-rent?

A. Jignesh Bhai pays Rs 2000 monthly for the electricity bills. The shop is owned by him only so there doesn’t exist any shop rent. There is also not any gas connection to pay for the same.

Q. What are the apps he uses for running his business?

A. Jignesh Bhai uses Tally ERP 9 for accounting purposes. Apart from this software, it uses only 2 applications i.e Google Pay & Phonepe. Lankavala Pannalal Chotalal is a small family-owned Kirana store currently operated by Jignesh Lankavala & his son. Jignesh Bhai is fully confident that his son would expand this business with his business skills.

About the Research

This research was conducted after an in-person interview with the merchant itself in local Gujarati slang. Later the whole conversation was transcribed into English & has expanded here.
