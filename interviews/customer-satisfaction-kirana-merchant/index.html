A Happy Customer is a Businessman’s Pride

    Backstory

    Tarun’s father opened this shop in 2001 when money was tight. He rented a place and started selling groceries and made just enough to support his family initially. Over the years, the shop flourished and earned a stable customer base that generated quite some revenue. Tarun’s elder brother, Akhil, did not want any part of the shop. Therefore, the responsibility to continue the legacy befell upon the younger one, and he took over the shop in 2011. Tarun wants to take full advantage of the power of technology and plans to transform his business into an online cartel.

    He lives in Kalkaji, Delhi with his wife and two daughters. His daughters, Priya and Aarti, study in a Government school nearby. Tarun’s elder brother Akhil, runs an electrical appliance shop in Malviya Nagar and lives with his family in Munirka. Tarun’s shop sells all grocery related products and some stationery items as well.

    Business

    1. - The business was first started in 2001 by Tarun’s father and he took over the responsibility in 2011.

    2. - His aim is to extend the business as much as he can and include more variety in his products.

    3. - Given the circumstances, he is planning to take the business online through various online platforms.

    4. - The main problem for him is that there are many grocery shops in that area, which increases the competition.

    Q: What is the name of your store?

    A: The shop is named ‘Aggarwal Store’, and we sell all grocery related products and some stationery items too.

    Q: Who are your family members?

    A: I am Tarun Aggarwal, 37 years old. I live in Kalkaji with my wife and 2 daughters, Priya(15) and Aarti(12) who are getting their education in a Government school not far from home.

    Q: Why and when did you start this business?

    A: My father started this business in 2001 in a shop on rent but now I own the shop. I took over the business in 2011 because I always believed that the shop is the love and blessings from my father.

    Q: What does your business deal with?

    A: I deal with all the grocery items that a household might need. I sell all grocery related products and some stationery items too.

    Q: What were the major difficulties you faced when you first started?

    A: As a retailer, my business suffers because people now-a-days prefer to buy products online. I am planning to take my business online through various online platforms as well. There is another shop named ‘Aggarwal Store’ that sells all grocery related products and some stationery items too which is a big competition. The main problem is that there are many grocery shops in this area, which increases the competition.

    Q: How much support have you hired?

    A: The store provides home delivery services also with the help of his two employees Nitiesh and Rahul. They both belong to the same family. Tarun also provides pick and drop facilities to his helpers. They have been working for over 5 years in this shop.

    Q: What is your role in the shop?

    A: I oversee my employees and manage the inventory in my shop. Besides, I also manage the billing counter, refill stocks, and deal with the wholesalers.

    Q: Who are your typical customers?

    A: I attend approximately 40-45 customers on an average daily basis. Among them 10-15 are my daily customers. Mainly, the customers are from the societies nearby. Various kinds of customers visit the store every day. From those sophisticated classes to the labourers, everyone is my customer.

    Q: What are your top priorities?

    A: My top priority is to deliver customer satisfaction. I believe my customers are God and they deserve a right bill. I do not distinguish between my customers and treat them with utmost respect. Because without them my shop would be nothing.

    Finance and Banking

    1. - The initial investment was made by Tarun’s father when he opened the shop and the exact amount is unknown.

    2. - He does not have a POS system in place because all his online transactions are made through UPI and QR while the rest 60% is made in cash.

    3. - He has not yet registered for GST and he does not have any tie up with any brand or associations either.

    Q: Do you use a POS Terminal for accepting payments, if yes, why, if no, why not?

    A: No I do not have a POS installed in my shop. Customers have the online mode of payment in place, therefore there is not much need of a POS.

    Q: What is the percentage of cash transactions at your shop?

    A: The percentage of cash transactions in my shop is around 60%.

    Q: Do you use an ATM? Why do you withdraw money from the ATM?

    A: I often use the ATM to withdraw cash for household purposes and paying my employees. I sometimes also use cash to settle small bills with the wholesalers.

    Q: How did you manage the initial capital?

    A: Tarun’s father started the business in 2001 in a shop on rent but now Tarun owns that shop. So he didn’t have to invest anything for starting the shop.

    Q: Have you registered for GST?

    A: Yes, I have registered for GST.

    Q: What is your monthly profit?

    A: After all the payments are done, I earn about 35,000-40,000 per month.

    Online Portal

    1. - He uses Google Pay and PhonePe for UPI and QR transactions.

    2. - He also uses a specialized billing software for accounting purposes.

    3. - Tarun spends his free time on applications like WhatsApp, Facebook, YouTube, and playing games on my phone.

    Q: Which apps do you use for running your business?

    A: I make the best use of technology to ease out the problems. I have in place UPI transactions, Google pay, phone pe, Pay tm as modes of payment available for the customers. I also have a billing machine at the store which helps in keeping track of all the transactions or expenses.

    Q: Do you accept payments through online portals and why?

    A: Yes I accept payment through applications like Pay tm, Google pay, Phone Pe. They have eased the problems of making or receiving a payment.

    Q: What do you do in your free time?

    A: During my free time in the shop I spend my time on applications like WhatsApp, Facebook, YouTube, playing games, or talking to my family during lunch time.
