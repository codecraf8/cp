A Woman’s Take on Business Tactics

    Backstory

    Shalini is a 45 years old woman who runs a general store in the Palem district of Visakhapatnam all by herself. Being a woman of the 20th century and starting a general store, she has seen a fair share of challenges. But no matter how difficult the situation, Shalini never succumbed to the hurdles life threw at her. From choosing her life partner to her occupation, she has made bold choices and lived by the struggles only to come out even stronger. Her shop is now one of the most visited shops in the locality and many depend upon her for their daily needs.

    She lives with her husband, a daughter, a son and their pet in a small rented house. Her store deals with all the groceries and other sundries like Rice, Dal, Flours, sugar/Jaggery, spices, vegetables, tea/Coffee, snacks and much more. Although she did not have to invest anything initially, she took some loan from the bank at the interest of 5.2% for expanding her shop.

    Business

    1. - Shalini owns the Sridevi kirana and General stores which is located in Palem, Visakhapatnam. The shop was handed over to her by her father.

    2. - Her store deals with all the groceries and other sundries like rice, dal, flours, sugar/Jaggery, spices and several other items.

    3. - She managed the initial capital by selling ancestral properties and for developing it she took the help of a bank.

    4. - The most challenging part of her business is that the income is not stable.

    Q: What is your name and age?

    A: My name is Shalini and my age is 45.

    Q: What is the name of the shop and locality?

    A: The name of my kirana shop is “Sridevi kirana and General stores” and the locality of that is Palem, Visakhapatnam, in Andhra Pradesh.

    Q: Why did you start this business?

    A: I am the only daughter of the family and the shop was handed over to me by my father. I have been overseeing the shop ever since.

    Q: Who are the members in your family?

    A: I live with my husband, daughter, son and a small pet in a rented house.

    Q: When did you start this business?

    A: I started this business long ago when I completed my intermediate.

    Q: What does your business deal with?

    A: My store deals with all the groceries and other sundries like Rice, Dal, Flours (Wheat Flour, Rice Flour, etc.),Sugar/Jaggery, Pices (Khada Masalas & Powdered Masalas), Vegetables (Onions & Potatoes), Tea/Coffee, Snacks and much more.

    Q: How did you manage the initial capital?

    A: I managed the initial capital with some help from my ancestral properties and for developing it I took help from a bank which provided loans.

    Q: What were the major difficulties in running the business?

    A: At times we face challenges like hiring employees whereas when the employee takes leave and in their absence it becomes difficult to manage both the store and family single handedly. Another challenge is that I cannot take any holidays without having some loss. Also there are ups and downs in the business such as when we get low profit compared to the peak seasons. For example we used to have good profit before the pandemic whereas now the business is too low and it is difficult to sustain.

    Q: Do you get help from your family members?

    A: No, there is no one to help me from my family member’s side. My parents and I are not in close contact and therefore there is no one to guide me or support here.

    Q: How much support have you hired?

    A: I have hired 1 girl named Kalpana and I have another servant at my home who also sometimes helps me out in my shop.

    Q: For the most of time you are in the shop what do you do?

    A: Usually I keep busy with handling things at the shop such as contacting the traders and managing the accounts. The first thing I do in the shop if I get any free time is looking after the inventory and stocks including sorting what goods need to be returned due to damage or the expiry of the products.

    Q: What are your top priorities?

    A: My top priority is to keep my customers happy. I sometimes go out of the way and provide free delivery in less than an hour for regular customers. Their satisfaction is my priority.

    Q: Who are your typical customers?

    A: My typical customers are the ones from in and around the locality of my shop. People mostly from the radius of 500 meters come to buy from my shop.

    Q: What do you do in your free time?

    A: I love listening to music and interacting with my employees and customers when I am free.

    Finance and Banking



    1. - She often uses the ATM for withdrawing money since it is safer to store money in banks than to carry it with her.

    2. - Her monthly income varies depending on the sales. It may be 30,000 or 3000.

    3. - She often takes out loans from the bank to run her business. She pays an interest rate of 5.2%.

    4. - She has registered for GST.

    Q: Do you use a POS Terminal for accepting payments, if yes, why, if no, why not?

    A: No, I do not use POS Terminal for accepting payments. I do not prefer to say.

    Q: What is the %age of cash transactions at your shop?

    A: Even in this pandemic, about 70% of the transactions are still made in cash.

    Q: What is the %age of QR code payments at your shop?

    A: In this pandemic season Covid-19 it is not more than 30% of the transactions.

    Q: Do you use an ATM? Why do you withdraw money from the ATM?

    A: Yes, I use an ATM frequently. Withdrawing money from the ATM is easier than carrying lumps of money with me wherever I go. It is better to withdraw the money whenever I need as it provides the service round the clock at any time at anyplace.

    Q: How much money do you make in a month?

    A: It varies every month. It can be 30,000/- per month or just 3000/-. So, I cannot say the exact figure, how much I earn in a month. Especially due to covid-19, business has been very unstable.

    Q: Did you take any loan from external people for running your shop? If yes, what is the interest you pay?

    A: External people yes, I often rely on banks for loans. I pay an interest of 5.12%

    Q: How do you repay his loan - UPI / Cash / Cheque / Online?

    Ans: I mostly pay it through UPI and sometimes cheque, descending whichever is more convenient.

    Q: Have you registered for GST?

    A: Yes I have registered for GST.

    Q: How much do you pay monthly for electricity, phone, gas, shop-rent?

    A: It is not the same exact amount every month and mostly depends on our usage. The bills of electricity, phone and gas differs from month to month. Besides I own the real estate, therefore there is no rent to be paid for it.

    Online portals

    1. - She accepts payment through online portals such as PayTM, Google Pay, PhonePe and Amazon Pay.

    Q: What are the apps you use for running your business?

    A: I use the apps Paytm, PhonePe, Gpay, and AmazonPay to ease transactions with my customers.

    Q: What apps do you use otherwise?

    A: I do not use any other application for my business other than the above mentioned apps.
