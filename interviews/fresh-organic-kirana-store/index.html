The ultimate destination of farm fresh food

    Backstory

    50 years old Nirav Chaudhary had to take an early retirement from his job when he met with an unfortunate accident. Being the only earning member, he decided to start a business in 2012 to improve his financial condition. Instead of just keeping the money from the provident fund in a safe box, Nirav decided to invest it in establishing his groceries store in Surat. The hardwork and risks paid off and soon his shop was the ultimate destination for the localities seeking fresh vegetables.

    Nirav lives with his family of five, wife, mother and two children. He keeps his profit margins low and vegetables fresh which ensures customer retention. His shop always has 2 to 3 customers waiting in line and a minimum sale of ₹5000/day. He uses the power of the internet to advertise his shop and reach new customers.

    Business

    1. - Nirav Chaudhary, started his business back in 2012 to improve his family’s financial condition after he took an early retirement.

    2. - His store has groceries at a comparatively low price with assured quality and quantity so he always has 2-3 waiting customers at his shop.

    3. - Nirav decided to invest the money from his provident fund in establishing his groceries store in Surat.

    4. - He has one person working under him to help him manage his shop.

    Q: Why did you start this business ?

    A: I started this business to live a better life and to improve my family’s financial condition. But the primary driving force was the will to give my kids a good education and therefore, a good life.

    Q: When did you start his business?

    A: I started the business back in 2012 when I took an early retirement from my 9-5 job. I was rendered weak from an unfortunate accident that made me unable to carry on with the travel. I had to voluntarily leave the job and that is when I started this business to provide for my family.

    Q: What does your business deal with?

    A: My shop primarily deals with vegetables and fruits. My store has groceries at a comparatively low price with assured quality and quantity so that ensures I always have 2-3 waiting customers in line.

    Q: What were the major difficulties in running the business?

    A: I faced many difficulties initially like not getting a single customer in a day but I didn’t give up. Instead I started working harder and smarter. I started advertising online, made door to door deliveries and gathered the trust of my customers eventually. I also face heavy competition from two bigger shops in the locality. Another challenge is handling the customers who typically bargain and are short tempered.

    Q: Do you get help from your family members? Have you hired any support?

    A: My family always supported me even though I wasn’t earning much initially. When I was out delivering groceries, my wife or mother took care of the store. However, now I earn enough to hire a support. I have one person working under me who helps me manage the shop.

    Q: For the most time in the shop, what do you do?

    A: Most of the time I am in the store I listen to music or complete the pending work. Sometimes interact with the customers, read newspapers, or keep the logs updated.

    Q: What are your top priorities and who are your typical customers?

    A: My top priorities are my family and my business. I try to keep the prices low but never compromise on the quality of my goods. My typical customers are the people from the nearby localities.

    Q: What do you do in your free time?

    A: In my free time I usually return home to spend time with my family, watch television or resort to the newspaper. I spend my off days with my family by picnicking, camping, and playing games.

    Finance and Banking

    1. - The initial investment for the store was gathered from the money that came from his provident fund. He has never taken a loan.

    2. - He owns multiple bank accounts and uses the ATM to withdraw cash for several payments and household needs.

    3. - He has a monthly income of around 25,000 and has registered for GST.

    Q: How did you manage the initial capital?

    A: I received a certain amount when I retired from my 9-5 job which I use to buy the store and set up my shop. Instead of just keeping the money from the provident fund in a safe box, I decided to invest it in establishing a source of income.

    Q: What is the percentage of cash transactions in your shop?

    A: The percentage of cash transactions in my shop is 40%. I also accept payments through POS and online portals.

    Q:Do you use an ATM? Why do you withdraw money from the ATM?

    A: I use an ATM to withdraw money for monthly household expenses which I credit from the profit. I also withdraw money to pay my staff and to settle other financial dealings.

    Q: How much money do you make in a month?

    A: I have a monthly income of around 25,000.

    Q: Do you take loans from external people for running your shop?

    A: I was fortunate enough to not have to take out any loan for all investments managed from the provident fund. I also keep a separate savings account to help me should any financial crisis arise.

    Q: Have you registered for GST?

    A: Yes, I have registered for GST.

    Q: How much do you pay monthly for electricity, phone, gas, and shop rent?

    A: My monthly expense of electricity, phone, and gas is around 5000. I own the shop, therefore do not have to pay any rent for it.

    Online Portals

    1. - Nirav keeps up with the technology to drive his competitive advantage. He uses both POS and QR to accept payments from customers.

    2. - He also uses social media to advertise his business to reach new customers and expand his business.

    3. - Whatsapp and Facebook are the two main applications that Nirav uses to manage his business.

    Q: What apps do you use to help you run the business?

    A: Social media advertising and promoting has helped me reach new customers and establish business. I depend on Whatsapp and Facebook to communicate with my customers and advertise my store.

    Q: What apps do you use otherwise?

    A: Besides Whatsapp and Facebook, I use the mail apps and music apps for communication and entertainment. I also use payment apps such as GooglePay, Paytm and PhonePe for QR and UPI transactions.

    Q: Do you use a POS terminal for accepting payments?Why or why not?

    A: Yes, I believe in keeping up with the changing scenarios of business can only bring me more opportunities and customers. Almost 20% of my daily transactions are dealt through the POS system.
