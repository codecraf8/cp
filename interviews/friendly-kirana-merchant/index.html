    Nitish Goyal, a 26 years old young man, runs the Radhey Shyam Departmental Store in the rustling bustling streets of Geeta Colony, East Delhi. Following the legacy of his father, Nitish continues to run the business his father started to make a living the only way he knew. He resides in a small house almost next to his shop with his parents and a younger sister. For nine years, Nitish and his father have been earning the bread by actually selling bread.

    Business

    1. - The dwellers of his locality are his regular customers who find him friendly and have kept coming back at the store over the years.

    2. - Nitish at times finds strength from his father and turns to him for any help, if needed, in the business.

    3. - Nitish keeps a record of the supplies and sales and his friendly countenance furthermore facilitates to keep the customers satiated with their demands.

    Q: What is your name and age?

    A: My full name is Nitish Goyal, and I am 26 years old.

    Q: What is the name of the shop and locality?

    A: The name of my shop is Radhey Shyam Departmental Store and it is located in the rustling bustling streets of Geeta Colony, East Delhi.

    Q: Who are the members of your family?

    A: I reside in a small house almost next to his shop with his parents and a younger sister.

    Q: Why did you start your business?

    A: My father started this business as a means of earning our livelihood. We hail from a business family for generations and my father had been trained in business tactics since childhood. He grew up aiming to be a businessman and so he did. In 2011, this departmental store found its existence in the busy streets of Geeta Colony catering to diverse grocery demands of a household and to the sweet tooth of the kids that visit the store as well.

    Q: What does your business deal with?

    A: We stock all FMCG items and packaged foods including milk products and soft drinks. We try to keep up with the demands of our customers by bringing in every product that a household might need and more. We also have stationery items and toys for kids.

    Q: What were the major difficulties in running your business?

    A: Running the business has not always been easy in the face of economic adversity. Earning regular customers was challenging in the initial business years. Even with the regular customers, times such as the demonetization and the pandemic affected our business. However, I have never succumbed to these business challenges.

    Q: Do you get help from your family members?

    A: Although, I manage the store entirely by myself, at times I find strength from my father and turn to him for any help, if needed, in the business.

    Q: Most of the time you are in the shop what do you do & What do you do in your free time?

    A: Most of my time in the store is spent catering to my customers. I am often engrossed in the calculations concerning stock and supplies in my free time. Otherwise I enjoy playing any mobile game on my phone sometimes.

    Q: What are your top priorities and who are your typical customers?

    A: My top priority is to keep my customers happy and meet their demands as much as possible. Most of my customers have been coming to my store for years. They are no more just my customers but have become my friends.

    Finance and Banking

    1. - Nitish’s father, Mr. Radhey Shyam used his savings from his old business to set up a new one.

    2. - Nitish doesn’t use POS Terminal for accepting payments as the traditional method of cash transactions is the only way they have known how to do business.

    3. - Since the demonetization, they have started accepting payments through Paytm but only through his personal account.

    4. - He hasn’t registered for GST. The electricity bills and the maintenance cost them 20,000 rupees a month on an average.

    Q: How did you manage the initial capital?

    A: My father, Radhey Shyam spent his hard-earned savings from his old business as the capital to set this store up where it stands now.

    Q: Do you use a POS Terminal for accepting payments, if yes, why, if no, why not?

    A: No I do not have a POS system in place. We are not comfortable in handling money through technology therefore did not install a card transaction system.

    Q: What is the percentage of cash transactions at your shop?

    A: We mostly prefer cash transactions and do not suggest our customers for online payment if there is cash payment is a viable option. Therefore our cash transactions range from 80 to 90% per day.

    Q: Do you use an ATM? Why do you withdraw money from the ATM?

    A: I have been to a bank but haven’t ever withdrawn money from an ATM myself. We use the cash for all sorts of payments and household errands.

    Q: How much money do you make in a month?

    A: On an average, the revenue that the business generates is 40,000 rupees a month. The revenue has mostly catered them enough to suffice their needs and saved them the trouble of having any debts.

    Q: Do you take any loans from external people for running your shop? If yes, what is the interest you pay?

    A: We have not taken any loan so far for any business requirements. We mostly use money from our revenue to settle all payments or to stock up our inventory.

    Q: Have you registered for GST?

    A: No I have not registered for GST.

    Q: How much do you pay monthly for electricity, phone, gas, and shop-rent?

    A: The electricity bills and the maintenance cost them 20,000 rupees a month on an average and a fixed amount of 399 rupees is spent in paying the monthly phone bills.

    Online Portals

    1. - He does not use any special app to run his business. He believes in the traditional ways of accounting and bookkeeping.

    2. - Nitish accepts payments through QR and UPI only when the customer seeks online assistance.

    Q: What are the apps you use for running your business?

    A: I use Paytm in case any customer wants to use the online portal for payment. Otherwise, there is no other app in use for managing our business.

    Q. What is the %age of QR code payments at your shop?

    A: The QR code payments are kept to bare minimum of 10 to 15% and are kept in place for occasional use. We believe in traditional ways of business where the cash system is the main mode of transaction.
