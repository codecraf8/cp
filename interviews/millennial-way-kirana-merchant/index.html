The Millennial Way of Business

    Backstory

    In the summer of 2001, Monu’s father lost his job under unexpected circumstances. Monu was only 10 years old when their family had fallen into a frenzy of economic turmoil. His father started desperately searching for jobs but with no success. This is when Monu’s grandfather suggested his son should start a business and build something of his own. The shop was set up in 2002 but the struggle was not over yet, in fact it had only begun. For the next 5 to 6 years his father struggled to set up a solid customer base.

    A few years later, Monu’s elder brother, Mohit completed his CA and guided them to expand their business among more categories like Milk Products, Frozen Foods, etc. 2 years later, after completing his BBA, Monu joined his father at the age of 24. 18 years later, their store has become one of the most sought out landmarks of the locality and they plan to branch out soon. He lives with his mother, father, brother and his wife in Shahdara, Delhi.

    Business

    1. - Monu’s father started this business with an initial investment of around INR 70,000 when he lost his job.

    2. - Monu joined the business after finishing his BBA when he was only 24 years old.

    3. - His brother is also tightly involved with the finance and taxation aspect of the business.

    4. - Their business goal is to expand their shop and open several branches.

    5. - The store usually sells all kinds of daily day to day life products like soaps, snacks, milk, etc but during occasions they add gift items like chocolates, Packed Juices, etc.

    Q: What is your name and age?

    A: I am Monu Chawla, 28 years old.

    Q: When did you start your business?

    A: This business was started by my father when I was 10 years old. We faced a lot of struggle in the beginning. However, after a period of 5 or 6 years, the business started to flourish.

    Q: Why did you start your business?

    A: My father lost his job when I was 9 years old. After looking desperately for jobs for a while, my grandfather suggested he start his own business. That’s when my father decided a general goods store was a decent place to start. After a few years, my elder brother completed his CA and guided us to expand the business.With time things started getting better and after completing my BBA I joined my father and now I run this business.

    Q: What does your business deal with?

    A: The store usually sells all kinds of daily day to day life products like soaps, snacks, milk, etc but during occasions they add gift items like chocolates, Packed Juices, etc. I am not connected with any association or brand. When we started to expand, we included categories like Milk Products, Frozen Foods, etc among others.

    Q: Who are your typical customers?

    A: The store caters to nearly the whole locality of more than 50 Houses and we also provide home delivery with negligible charges.

    Q: What was the initial investment made?

    A: It’s quite hard to tell about the initial investment but it was around Rs 70,000.

    Q: How do you accept orders?

    Customers usually text us their orders via whatsapp or Calls us where our staff replies and delivers their goods. We accept orders from 10 am to 2 pm then all the goods will be delivered till 7pm. Q: Will you try to bring your business online? A: Yes, we will definitely try to bring our business online in upcoming years but currently we are focusing on expanding our business by opening more branches across delhi then we’ll all try cross chain delivery.

    Q: What were the major difficulties in running your business?

    A: The major difficulties that my father faced when he started this business is the competition from the neighbouring shops. He also struggled to create a strong customer base in the beginning. But after a period of 5 to 6 years his struggle paid off and the shop started flourishing. The shop expanded further when my brother completed his CA and guided us better.

    Q: Do you get help from your family members?

    A: Since I was passed on the responsibility of the shop, it did not have to face the initial struggle. However, I seek business advice from my brother wherever in need. Besides, my brother also looks after the taxation and other financial aspects.

    Q: How much support have you hired?

    A: The store currently holds 3 boys. One for delivery and two for sales and management. Q: What is your long term business goal? A: Our Business Goal is to provide best customer support with a valid price. We are continuously adopting and adding new goods and stuff to our business which makes us unique from other local businesses. We always appreciate healthy competition as it helps us to evolve with time.

    Finance and Banking

    1. - The team is continuously working on expanding among multiple categories and planning to open a branch very soon.

    2. - Currently their priority is to provide the best customer service.

    3. - Due to the coronavirus pandemic they saw a 30% rise in online transaction and home deliveries.

    Q: What is your preferred mode of transaction?

    A: Usually customers prefer cash over online payment platforms like PayTM, PhonePe, etc but they support all kinds of payment methods.

    Q: Do you take any loans from external people for running your shop? If yes, what is the interest you pay?

    A: Usually we don’t need any loan to expand our business, we just reinvest 70% of whatever we earn. As I said we are planning to expand our business so we might take a loan of Rs 25 Lakh at as minimum interest as possible.

    Q: How do you handle the taxation and ITRs

    A: Taxation and ITRs are quite smooth as my brother is CA and he handles all these stuffs.

    Q: How much do you pay monthly for electricity, phone, gas, and shop-rent?

    A: We pay approximately Rs 20,000 for other expenses like electricity, maintenance, etc. The shop is owned by us, therefore there is no rent to be paid.

    Q: How much do you pay monthly to your employees?

    A: We pay approximately Rs 50,000 per month to our staff including incentives.

    Q: How much profit do you make in a year?

    A: Our current annual revenue is like 60-70 lakh per annum from whom we make a profit of approximately 20 lakh per annum.

    Online Portals

    1. - To keep up with the changing times, Monu stays in touch with all digital platforms.

    2. - They use a softwares to mitigate human errors in billing and data handling.

    3. - They accept payment from their customers through various modes including POS, QR and UPI.

    Q: How do you adapt to the upcoming technological trends?

    A: We upgrade ourselves with time. We accept all sorts of digital payment, use softwares for data handling, customer support, and billing.

    Q: Do you accept payment through Online Portals?

    A: We accept all sorts of digital payment including POS, QR and UPI.
