The essence of quality before quantity

    Shivnath Agarwal, aka Bablu aged 43 , rules the market of Tagore Garden shopping complex in West Delhi through his supreme quality kirana store. Agarwal Kirana store popular as Bablu Ki Dukan is the ultimate destination for traditional quality kirana items ranging from spices to toiletries to packaged food and much more for the localites. Shivnath has remained loyal to the standards of quality set by his father and intends to keep it up.

    Shivnath is a married man. He lives with his wife Shashi Agarwal, his 7 years son Shiv, and his father Om Prakash Agarwal. Agarwal Kirana store is more like a family heritage for Bablu and is run by him and his wife under his father’s guidance.

    Let’s take a sneak peak into his store!

    Business

    1. - Shivnath inherited the business from his father, who managed to run the business for 15 years. He has always prioritised maintaining the same level of customer trust and loyalty as his father.

    2. - The major difficulty he faces today is surviving amongst the modern departmental stores which provide huge goods at discounted prices and offers.

    3. - The Agarwal Store deals with all sorts of kirana items as well as consumer goods such as spices, condiments, flour, toiletries, packaged food, and several others.

    Q: Why did you start this business ?

    A: I inherited the store from my father Om Prakash ji, who had run the business for almost 15 years before he retired. Although I pursued a BA degree, I was always keen on taking over the business. I have always put my effort in maintaining the same level of customer trust and loyalty as my father.

    Q: When did you start his business?

    A: My father inaugurated the shop in 1994 and has invested 15 years to build its reputation. 12 years ago my father decided to retire and vested upon me the responsibility of the shop. I started my journey as a businessman in 2008.

    Q: What does your business deal with?

    A: My business deals with all sorts of kirana items as well as consumer goods such as spices, condiments, flour, toiletries, packaged food and several others.

    Q: What were the major difficulties in running the business?

    A: The major difficulties which my father faced were establishing a permanent footing in the market complex among many other kirana stores, which he attained by sticking to the quality of the products more than anything else. The major difficulty I face today is surviving amongst the modern departmental stores which provide huge goods at discounted prices and offers. However, I retain the trust of my customers by following my father’s values - never to compromise with quality.

    Q: Do you get help from your family members? Have you hired any support?

    A: Yes, The store is run and maintained by me and my wife along with the help of my cousins. My father guides me with all financial and business situations as well. I have two employees, Yadav and Suresh who look after the home delivery of big orders. Apart from that my wife and two of my cousin brothers look after the shop along with me.

    Q: For the most time in the shop, what do you do?

    A: I spend almost 10 hours a day in the shop. For most of the shopkeeping hours, I am engaged with ongoing customers, even during the afternoons. Besides this, I tally the accounts, interact with my wife, brothers, and sometimes the customers. Also, if time permits, I go through the newspapers.

    Q: What are your top priorities and who are your typical customers?

    A: My top priority is to maintain the quality of the goods I sell, even if it calls for slightly higher prices than usual. Because that, I believe will always retain the trust of my customers. The typical customers of my shop include the residents of the locality and the nearby areas.

    Q: What do you do in your free time?

    A: In my free time I usually return home to spend time with my son or watch television.

    Finance and Banking

    1. - The initial investment for the store was gathered by Shivnath’s father from multiple relatives along with a minimum savings of his grandfather. However, there were no formal loans involved in the initial funding.

    2. - He owns multiple bank accounts and uses the ATM to withdraw cash for several payments and household needs.

    3. - He has never had to take any loan from the bank and has managed to cover expenses during all difficult times by his savings.

    4. - He is in the final procedure for registering for GST

    Q: How did you manage the initial capital?

    A: Since it was my father who started the business, I reckon the initial capital was borrowed from multiple relatives along with the minimum savings of his grandfather. However, no formal loans were involved in the initial funding.

    Q: What is the percentage of cash transactions in your shop?

    A: The percentage of cash transactions in my shop is 100%, as I only deal with on the spot cash payments.

    Q:Do you use an ATM? Why do you withdraw money from the ATM?

    A: Yes, I use an ATM. I withdraw money from the ATM for attaining cash to buy the products from the wholesaler, clearing the payments of my staff and for my household expenses as well.

    Q:How much money do you make in a month?

    A: I manage to make around 60000 to 70000 in a month at present.

    Q: Do you take loans from external people for running your shop?

    A: No, I have been fortunate to never have to take external loans to run the shop. I have always maintained a separate savings account to support us during the difficult times and have managed to suffice with the savings.

    Q: Have you registered for GST?

    A: Yes, I am in the final procedure for registering for GST.

    Q: How much do you pay monthly for electricity, phone, gas, and shop rent?

    A: I pay around Rs 2000/ month for electricity, and Rs 2500/ month for phone. There is no gas expense at the shop and I own the area, thus, no shop rent either.

    Online Portals

    1. - He does not accept payments through online portals because he is not digitally fluent.

    2. - He relies on traditional ways of transactions to settle all payments.

    3. - He uses Whatsapp and Facebook for communication and entertainment.

    Q: What apps do you use to help you run the business?

    A: I still rely on the traditional business methods and only use Whatsapp for interactions with dealers and customers.

    Q: What apps do you use otherwise?

    A: I use Whatsapp and Facebook only to socialize or for entertainment.

    Q: Do you use a POS terminal for accepting payments?Why or why not?

    A: No, I do not use a POS terminal for accepting payments for the simple reason that I only prefer one mode of payment that is Cash payment. I am not very digitally fluent and feel comfortable dealing with cash transactions only.
