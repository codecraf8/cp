A Quaint Shop in a Quaint Town

    Mr. Jagjeevan Dusadh

    Backstory

    Mr. Jagjeevan was desperately searching for a job when he was fired from the last one and was in dire financial trouble. One of his close friends suggested he start a business instead which would give him freedom from being bossed around and bring in money if he did it right. He began his journey as a businessman in 2008 and put his heart and soul to it. Soon his business started blooming and he has never had to look back since then.

    Mr. Jagjeevan Dusadh is the owner of Dusadh cosmetics and Store, a kirana store in the town of Mahwah, Rajasthan. He lives with his wife and two kids. His family is his top priority. He works hard to provide his children with the best possible education.

    Business

    1. -The major reason behind starting this business was that he was fired from his former place of work. As a means of earning his livelihood, Mr, Jagjeevan resorted to business.

    2. - His shop deals with all cosmetic products and everyday supplies.

    3. - He gets help from his son for managing the shop regularly. However, during the festive season they hire two helpers to manage the extra workload.

    Q: What is your name? How old are you?

    A: My name is Jagjeevan Dusadh. I am currently 50 years old, and will turn 51 in a couple of months.

    Q: Can you tell me the name of the shop and the locality in which it is situated?

    A: Well, my store’s name is Dusadh Cosmetics and Store. It is located on Tehsil road, near Ganesh Chowk.

    Q: Why did you start this business?

    A: The major reason behind starting this business was that I was fired from my former place off work. So, for a livelihood, I was desperately searching for a job. That is when my friend recommended that I should start a business instead.

    Q: Who are the members of your family?

    A: I live with my wife, my daughter and my son.

    Q: Did you start your business?

    A: As far as I remember, it was in 2008. it has been more than ten years since I started.

    Q: What does your business deal with? Who are your typical customers?

    A: You can find a variety of products at our store, ranging from cosmetics to everyday supplies. Most of our customers are people living in the same locality.

    Q: What were the major difficulties that you faced while running the business?

    A: You are well aware, Mahwa is a small town. Hence, initially it was difficult getting all the necessary supplies and the major difficulty arose due to the current pandemic. I could not open the store and even when I did the business was not nearly as good.

    Q: Get any help from your family members? Have you hired any support?

    A: Yes, my son usually helps me both at the store and outside. He manages my accounts and helps the deliveries as well. I do have one worker here. My son helps a lot of times and during festive seasons, we usually hire two people to manage the extra load of work.

    Q: What do you do for the majority of the time while at the shop? What are your top priorities?

    A: If there are no customers at the shop, I usually watch TV. Also, I usually invest in stocks as that is an additional way to earn money. so most of the time is spent on my phone or in front of the TV. My family, of course, is my top priority. Be able to provide my kids with the best possible education. I just want my kids to get settled.

    Q: What do you do in your free time?

    A: I usually monitor the market for the rates. Besides that, I also like to watch the news.

    1. - The initial investment for the store was gathered from relatives at an interest rate of 2% and the rest was managed with Mr. Jagjeevan’s own savings.However, there were no formal loans involved in the initial funding.

    2. - He owns multiple bank accounts and uses the ATM to withdraw cash for several payments and household needs.

    3. - He often takes loans from his relatives for business purposes but not from the bank.

    Q: How did you manage the initial capital?

    A: I did have some savings. but I also had to take a loan from my relatives at 2% interest rate.

    Q: What is the percentage of cash transactions at your shop?

    A: A lot of people pay in cash. I would say about 50%.

    Q: What is the percentage of Q R code transactions at your shop?

    A: That would be the rest of the people. All these people pay via Paytm or phonepay.

    Q: Do you use the ATM? Why do you withdraw money from the ATM?

    A: I do use an ATM. I find it convenient to deal with cash for my household errands.

    Q: How much do you make in a month approximately?

    A: Usually, I earn upto 50000 rupees per month. But there is no certain amount that I can provide you with. there are too many variables., the earnings increase significantly. Recently due to the pandemic, the business has suffered a lot.

    Q: Do you take loans to run your shop? if yes, what is the rate of interest?

    A: I do take loans sometimes, mostly from relatives. The rate of interest is 2%.

    Q: How do you prepare your loans – UPI/ cheque / cash / online?

    A: I prefer to use a check. but sometimes I also make online transactions.

    Q: Have you registered for GST?

    A: Yes, I have.

    Q: How much do you pay monthly for electricity, phone and rent?

    A: The rent per month is 2000 rupees, plus thousand rupees electricity. The phone bill is 800 rupees per month.

    Online Portals

    1. - Mr. Jagjeevan accepts online payment portals such as UPI and QR codes for transactions in his shop.

    2. - He does not have a POS system in his shop since his customers are not aware of such systems.

    Q: What are the apps that you use for running your business?

    A: I use Paytm and phonepay.

    Q: What apps do you use otherwise?

    A: I do use Amazon, WhatsApp and other apps people normally use.

    Q: Do you use a POS terminal for accepting payments? If yes, why, if not, why not?

    A: No, I do not use a POS terminal for accepting payments. Mawha is a small town. People are not really aware of these things.
