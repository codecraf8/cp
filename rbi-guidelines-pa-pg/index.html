RBI Guidelines for Payment Aggregators and Gateways

    Introduction

    After taking into account the important functions of the intermediaries in the online payments space, the Reserve Bank of India has decided to regulate in entirety the activities of PAs and provide baseline technology-related recommendations to PGs. The RBI has recently issued guidelines for regulating both Payment Gateways & Payment Aggregators.

    On 17 March 2020, the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) issued Guidelines on Regulation of Payment Aggregators (PA) and Payment Gateways (PG) .

    These entities, including fintech startups such as PayU, Instamojo, Paytm, CCAvenue, Razorpay and BillDesk, accept and facilitate online transactions.

    The guidelines have been issued under Section 18 of the Payment and Settlement Systems Act 2007 and shall come into effect from April 1, 2020. The guideline details governance, shareholding, technology, security and operational requirements all payment aggregators and gateways need to comply with.

    Definitions

    The RBI stated that payment gateways (PGs) are entities that also help process transactions carried out by digital commerce websites and other merchants, but without merchants having to create their own payment integration system. Payment Gateways are entities that provide technology infrastructure to route and facilitate processing of an online payment transaction without handling the funds. Examples of such service providers include Billdesk, CCAvenue, Firstdata, and Ingenico.

    Payment Aggregators are entities that facilitate e-commerce sites and merchants to accept various payment instruments from the customers for completion of their payment obligations.

    RBI Guidelines for Payment Aggregators

    The guideline mandates PAs to obtain authorisation from the RBI and prescribes detailed technology and operational guidelines such as merchant onboarding, data sovereignty, customer data access, audit obligations, etc.

    RBI has asked the payment aggregators to adhere to the security guidelines and KYC and AML( Anti Money Laundering) rules. PAs are advised to gather adequate information and data security infrastructure and systems to prevent frauds.

    New PAs to have a minimum net worth of INR 15 crores at the time of application for authorisation and shall attain a net worth of INR 25 crores by the end of the third financial year of grant of authorisation. This shall be maintained at all times thereafter.

    Existing non-bank PAs have to obtain authorisation from the Department of Payment System and Settlement, RBI by 30 June 2021.

    To enhance the security of ATMs, RBI has prohibited Payment Aggregators from allowing online transactions to be done with ATM pin as the second factor of authentication.

    Furthermore, e-commerce marketplaces providing PA services would be separated as an entity and would be identified as technology service providers or outsourcing partners' for banks or non-banks.

    Existing PAs to achieve a net worth of INR 15 crores by 31 March 2021 and a net worth of INR 25 crores by the end of the third financial year, i.e., on or before 31 March 2023, and shall be maintained at all times thereafter.

    According to the guidelines, E-commerce marketplaces providing PA services would be separated as an entity and would be identified as technology service providers or outsourcing partners' for banks or non-banks.

    PAs shall undertake background and antecedent check of merchants to ensure that such merchants do not have any malafide intention.

    Non-bank PAs shall maintain the amount collected by them in an escrow account with any scheduled commercial bank. The obligation to settle a transaction has been clearly outlined in the guideline.

    RBI has also asked PAs to set up designated nodal offices to deal with issues of customers.
